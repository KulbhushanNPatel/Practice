Delhi Chat

Ingredients: 5 medium size potato boiled & peeled 1 tbsp butter 1/2 tsp oil 1/2 tbsp chat masala 1 +1/4 cup tomatoes chopped 2 tbsp coriander leaves chopped 5-6 green chilies chopped 1 tbsp ginger grated 1/4 tbsp chat masala salt to taste 1 tbsp red chilli powder 1/2 tsp lemon juice 1 tbsp butter 2 tbsp onion chopped 1/2 tsp black salt Kala namak 1/4 tsp black pepper powder 1 tsp roasted cumin powder 1/4 tbsp chat masala 1 tbsp lemon juice 2 tbsp coriander leaves chopped 2 tbsp sev or namkeen
Frying The Potatoes :
Collect all the required ingredients. Cut the boil potatoes into cubes. In a pan, heat 1/2 tsp oil, 1 tbsp butter, and 1/2 tbsp chat masala on low flame. Add potato pieces and fry till crisp and golden. Do not over mix, as the potatoes will break apart. you can fry potatoes in one or two batches depending on the size of the pan or kadai. Saute the potatoes till they turn golden brown from all the sides and it may take 8-10 minutes to get the crunchy texture. keep on turning the potatoes as required for even frying. Transfer to a plate and keep aside.
Preparing Masala:
Clean the pan with tissue paper and in the same pan, heat 1 tbsp butter. Add grated ginger and saute its raw smell disappears. Then add green chilies and saute it for few seconds. Now add tomatoes and cook for a minute on low flame. After a minute, add salt, red chili powder, 1/4 tbsp chat masala, and 1/2 tsp lemon juice. Mix well and add chopped coriander leaves. Again mix everything very well. With a slotted spoon, mash the tomato mixture so that there are no lumps and it looks like a paste or chutney. Turn off the flame. Masala is ready for chaat.
Making Aloo Chaat:
When the potatoes are still hot, take them in a mixing bowl. Add the prepared masala. Also add all the spice powders like roasted cumin powder, black salt, black pepper powder, and 1/4 tbsp chat masala. You can adjust spice powders as per your requirements. Then adds 1 tbsp lemon juice or add as per taste. Toss and mix everything very well, so that the prepared masala, spice powders and lemon juice is evenly coated on the fried potatoes. Add some chopped onion and coriander leave to make it tastier. mix gently without breaking the aloo/potato pieces. place the aloo chaat in a serving bowl. While serving aloo chaat you can garnish with some pomegranate arils if you want. Top with the generous amount of onion and sev. Spicy and tempting Aloo Chaat is ready. Garnish it with green coriander and serve it as a snack or a starter snack. Relish the amazing flavors of Aloo Chaat to satisfy your cravings.

Mexican Salad

Ingredients
For the Mexican Salad:
1 can black beans
1 1/2 cups corn (canned or frozen)
2 cups cherry tomatoes, cut into halves
1 cup bell pepper, cut into medium-sized pieces
1 avocado, cut into medium-sized pieces
1/2 cup cilantro, chopped
1 red onion, finely chopped
or the Dressing;
1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
2 tablespoons olive oil
1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
1 teaspoon paprika powder
1 splash maple syrup
1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
2 tablespoons water
1-2 cloves of garlic, minced
black pepper, to taste
salt, to taste
1 lb fresh or dried white noodles
medium thickness 1 small bunch leafy greens (spinach, bok choy, or choy sum) chopped peanuts chopped scallion (optional) Instructions:

Rinse and drain the black beans and the corn. Cut the cherry tomatoes into halves and the avocado and the bell pepper into medium-sized pieces. Finely chop the cilantro. Chop the red onion.
Combine all ingredients in a large bowl. Make the dressing: Combine all ingredients in a small bowl and stir until combined.
Pour the dressing over the vegetables and beans and stir well. Enjoy!

Dan Dan Noodles

2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
1 inch-long piece of Chinese cinnamon (gui-pi)
2 star anise
1 cup oil
1/4 cup crushed red pepper flakes
3 teaspoons oil
8 oz. ground pork
2 teaspoons sweet bean sauce
2 teaspoons shaoxing wine
1 teaspoon dark soy sauce
1/2 teaspoon five spice powder
1/3 cup sui mi ya cai
In a small pot, add the Sichuan peppercorns, cinnamon stick, star anise, and oil. Over medium low heat, slowly heat to 325 degrees Fahrenheit, and then turn off the heat. Wait 6-7 minutes, then remove the peppercorns, cinnamon stick, and star anise with a slotted spoon.
Add the crushed red pepper flakes and allow them to steep in the hot oil. It should start smelling fragrant, almost like popcorn. Allow the oil to cool. This makes more chili oil than you’ll need, but you’ll be glad to have it on hand for use in other dishes. Store in a glass jar and keep refrigerated.
In a wok, heat a teaspoon of oil over medium heat, and brown the ground pork. Add the sweet bean sauce, shaoxing wine, dark soy sauce, and five spice powder. Cook until all the liquid is evaporated. Set aside. Heat the other 2 teaspoons of oil in the wok over medium heat, and sautee the sui mi ya ci (pickled vegetables) for a few minutes. Set aside.

The Sauce

2 tablespoons sesame paste (tahini)
3 tablespoons soy sauce
2 teaspoons sugar
1/4 teaspoon five spice powder
1/2 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorn powder (we ground whole Sichuan peppercorns in a mortar and pestle)
1/2 cup of your prepared chili oil (scary, but yes!)
2 cloves garlic, very finely minced
¼ cup hot cooking water from the noodles
Mix together all the ingredients. Taste and adjust seasoning if you like. You can loosen it with more hot water, add more Sichuan peppercorn powder, etc.
Cook the noodles according to package directions and drain. We used fresh noodles, but dry noodles are great too. I actually might prefer the dried, because they’re a bit less starchy. (Now would be the time to add some of the noodle cooking water to your sauce). Blanch the greens in the noodle water, and drain.
